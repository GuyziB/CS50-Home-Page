Sports Enthusiast
Couch Expert Thoughts

Over the years, the sports enthusiast in me has changed. That's natural I suppose. I used to play and watch it all. I loved it all, except soccer. I have never been able to watch soccer. Sure the big occasions, everyone watches. And live, soccer is certainly more entertaining than on the TV. But no, that's a tricky one for me. It's always on, and I really don't like watching it.

So these days of wall to wall sport...14 channels here in South Africa. There are so many space filling options that are just rubbish. Match up for match up sakes. I have started to cut back on the viewing. (The generalist coming through again!) I love watching cycling. Precentage wise, I have gone from 0% about 10 years back to at most opportunities these days. I love the one day classics, mountain days in the grand tours and the road world champs. Love the downhill mountain biking and event track championships.

I love watching the big events. The swimming at the olympics, rugby world cup, mens 100m at a world champs. When reputations are at stake, I'm in.

The golf majors are also cool. Tricky though, because I live in South Africa and only The Open is within our timezone. One of my great sporting memories is during my run from Johannesburg to Cape Town, the masters 'Monday' was on. 2019 - Tiger won. I was on this great adventure to Cape Town; my own challenge of sorts. Then there is Tiger making one of the greatest comebacks in sport. The following vehicle with my teammates shouting that Molinari was imploding. I got back into the combi and a non-golfer carried on running while we watched the last 9 holes. What a day that was!
